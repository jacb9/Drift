DRIFT
The word is moving. Pin it down.
How to play
What is Drift?
Drift is a word-guessing game like Wordle — with one difference. The hidden word is alive. After each guess, it may shift one letter to escape you. You have 6 guesses to pin it down before it gets away.
Basic rules
Guess a valid 5-letter word. After each guess you get colour feedback, then the word may drift. You win by getting all five letters correct on the same guess — before the word escapes.
What do the colours mean?
Green — correct letter, correct position.

Amber — letter is in the word, wrong position.

Grey — letter is not in the word.
How does drifting work?
After you guess, the word looks for a neighbouring word — one letter different — that undoes your progress. It prefers moves that break your green letters or remove your yellow letters. If it finds a good escape route, it takes it. The drift log tells you whether the word moved, and if so, which position changed.
What happens to the board when the word drifts?
The board is frozen history — it always shows the feedback you received at the time of each guess, against whatever the word was then. It does not update after a drift. Use it as an audit trail of what you learned.
What happens to the keyboard when the word drifts?
The keyboard updates immediately to reflect the current word. It replays all your past guesses against the new target and shows what they would mean now. A letter that was green may go grey. A letter that was grey may come back as yellow or green. The keyboard is live intelligence — the board is history.
What is the PIN meter?
PIN shows the percentage of positions you have locked down with confirmed green letters across all your guesses. A higher pin score means the word has fewer escape routes. At 100% you have the word cornered — but it may still drift one last time if it can.
Does the word always drift?
No. The word only drifts when it can find a neighbour that genuinely improves its position — breaking a green or removing a yellow. Early guesses with no hits give it nothing to escape from. As you close in, it gets more desperate. Late in the game the drift probability drops slightly, but a cornered word is still dangerous.
Strategy tips
Watch the keyboard after every drift, not just the board. A letter that resurrects from grey is a free clue. Prioritise locking multiple positions at once — the word can only change one letter per drift, so two or three confirmed greens in the same guess are hard to escape simultaneously. The drift log tells you which position moved — combined with the keyboard, you can often deduce the new letter.
